About SafeRelate

SafeRelate is built on the NeuroRelational Method (NRM), an evidence-based framework that integrates neuroscience, attachment theory, and relational psychology.

Our approach recognizes that lasting change requires emotional safety as its foundation. We provide assessment tools and insights that help individuals, families, and organizations understand the relational patterns that either support or inhibit growth.

Through comprehensive assessments, we identify the six core domains that determine relational readiness—giving you clarity on where you are and what needs attention before meaningful change can take root.

SafeRelate™ Assessments

SafeRelate™ assessments are evidence-informed, non-diagnostic profiling tools that measure safety, emotional SafeRelate regulation, relational readiness, and structural containment, the conditions required for learning, healing, and behaviour change

Evidence-Informed Framework

Grounded in Psychological & Neuroscientific Research

SafeRelate™ integrates established research into one coherent regulation-based model.

Understanding how early attachment patterns shape relational behavior and emotional regulation across the lifespan.

Exploring the neural mechanisms that underlie social connections and emotional processing.

Applying polyvagal theory to understand autonomic nervous system responses in relational contexts.

Evidence-based approaches to understanding and developing emotional regulation capacities.

Understanding how family dynamics and systemic patterns influence individual wellbeing and relational health.

Six Core Domains

Our assessments evaluate the essential dimensions that determine relational health and readiness for change.

Emotional Safety

The foundational capacity to feel secure, seen, and supported in relationships.

Attachment Imprints

Early relational patterns that shape expectations, behaviors, and connection styles.

Emotion Regulation

The ability to recognize, tolerate, and modulate emotional responses in healthy ways.

Relational Readiness

Capacity to engage authentically and adapt within relationships and systems.

Structural Containment

The external and internal frameworks that provide stability, predictability, and boundaries.

Corrective Readiness

The developmental stage and openness required for transformation and healing.
